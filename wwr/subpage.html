Five Hiking Trails You Must Visting in Eastern Kentucky

Double Arch, Courthouse Rock and Auxier Ridge

Courthouse Rock near Stanton, kentucky. It takes 1 hour and 20 min to drive from Lexington to this beautiful location. This trail is very friendly to hiking beginners. With a total length of a 6.0-mile, most hikers can finish the trail in less than 3 hours. This trail is a loop such that there is no worry about getting lost in the mountain. It will be very comfortable for families to spend a wonderful afternoon in this beautiful trail.

Natural Bridge

Natural bridge is in Slade, Kentucky. It takes about 45 mintes driving from Lexington. This 0.75-mile long trail is full of a lot green. This provides a lot of comfortable shades along the path in the summer. In the fall, the natural bridge trail is one of the best places in Kentucky for seeing the red leaves.

Breaks Interstate Park Loop

Breaks Interstate Park locates at the state boundary between Kentucky and Virginia. This trail is 4.7-mile long. This beautiful loop has the nickname as "Grand Canyon of the south". This is a great hike for spending a great afteroon with your family in this hike.

Eagle Falls

Eagle Falls is near the Honeybee, Kentucky. This beautiful trail is only 1.15-mile long but you can admire one of the most breath-taking falls in the eastern Kentucky. It is a great trail for camping with your family. There is also a well-known resturant next to th trail named Moonbow Cafe. You can get the best sandwich from Moonbow Cafe for really affordable prices

Pinnacle Trail

The Pinnacle Trail is in the Cumberland Gap National Park. Thhis 7.8-mile trail leads to the Pinnacle Overlook. From there you can see the mountain range in the eastern Kentucky. When it is Fall, the Pinnacle trail will become a colorful scenery and you will see the best leaves in the south east side of the nation. This a great trail for your Fall break week.
